GAA Nanosheet (NS) FETs are prime candidates for sub-3nm neuromorphic Spiking Neural Network (SNN) hardware accelerators. Because SNNs achieve power efficiency through temporal sparsity, the Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) neurons must consume minimal energy during spikes. This dashboard synthesizes empirical and TCAD results from 7 pioneering papers (17 distinct configurations), exploring how gate spacer engineering (dielectric constant, dual-k structures, vacuum air-gaps) modulates transistor leakage (I_OFF), driving capability (I_ON), subthreshold swing (SS), and DIBL. These device metrics are subsequently mapped to system-level SNN constraints: **Energy per spike (fJ)**, **firing latency**, and **MNIST accuracy**.

Key Multi‑Paper Findings & Future Co‑Design Directions
  • Electrostatic Modulation via Spacer Dielectrics: Symmetrical inner spacers utilizing medium-to-high k oxides (like $HfO_2$ or $TiO_2$) provide outstanding subthreshold gate controls, maximizing $I_{\text{ON}}$ and minimizing SS. However, they dramatically increase parasitic capacitances.
  • Dual-k and Air-Gap spacer emergence: Integrating dual-k inner spacers (e.g. low-k air gaps on outer portions combined with high-k inner gates) decouples the drive-leakage trade-off, enabling SNNs with both high accuracy and minimal spiking energies (down to 0.5 - 0.8 fJ).
  • SNN Spike Energy & Temperature constraints: Spiking architectures operating under severe environments (up to 125°C) face multi-fold increments in off-state transistor leakage. Co-design strategies demand aggressive $V_{\text{TH}}$ tailoring to maintain system-level SNN power envelopes.
  • Future Directions: Exploration of low-dimensional 2D transition metal dichalcogenide (TMD) channels with air-gap spacers, and neuromorphic circuit integration utilizing hybrid analog-digital integrate-and-fire systems.
